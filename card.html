Komodo Dragon

This lizard's large, curved and serrated teeth are its deadliest weapon, tearing flesh with efficiency. The tooth serrations hold bits of meat from its most recent meal, and this protein-rich residue supports large numbers of bacteria. Some 50 different bacterial strains, at least seven of which are highly septic, have been found in the saliva. Researchers have also documented a venom gland in the dragon's lower jaw. In addition to the harmful bacteria, the venom prevents the blood from clotting, which causes massive blood loss and induces shock.

  • Habitat: Komodo dragons are named after the Island of Komodo off of Indonesia. the prefer hot, dry climates.
  • Life Span: Komodo dragons have an average life span of 30 years.
  • Reproduction: Females lay upto 30 eggs, they dig a hole about 30 feet deep and lay their eggs.
  • Food: Monkeys, goats, wild boars, and most favorite deer. Komodo dragons are efficient eaters, leaving behind only about 12 percent of their prey. They eat bones, hooves and sections of hide, as well as intestines.

Komodo dragons can grow to an 10 feet long, and can weigh up to 150 lbs. when fully grown!
